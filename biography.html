'A versatile and charismatic performer', Jeremy Kleeman is a graduate of the Royal College of Music, a 2023 Samling Artist and a Melba Opera Trust Sambrook Scholar.

Recent performances include the title role in Le nozze di Figaro for State Opera South Australia, Guglielmo in Così fan tutte with Opera Queensland, and for the State Opera South Australia 'Gilbert & Sullivan Festival', Captain Corcoran in HMS Pinafore and Sergeant of Police in The Pirates of Penzance.

Jeremy is known for his creation of roles in several world premiere operas, most recently performing Reverend Stratton and Mr. Jeffris in Elliott Gyger and Pierce Wilcox’s bold adaptation of Peter Carey’s Oscar and Lucinda for Sydney Chamber Opera. In 2017, Jeremy premiered ‘Husband’ in Hing- yan Chan’s Double Happiness for the Canberra International Music Festival and in 2016, was an original cast member of George Palmer’s adaptation of the Tim Winton classic Cloudstreet with State Opera of South Australia, creating the roles of Toby Raven and Gerry Clay. The same year, Jeremy earned Helpmann and Green Room Award nominations for his performance of Magus in the new Baroque pastiche Voyage to the Moon, with libretto by Michael Gow and musical curation by Alan Curtis. And in 2013, Jeremy created the role Albert the Pudding in Calvin Bowman and Anna Goldsworthy’s The Magic Pudding — The Opera for Victorian Opera.

In 2024, Jeremy will create the title role in Jack Symonds and Louis Garrick's new opera Gilgamesh for Sydney Chamber Opera and Opera Australia.
